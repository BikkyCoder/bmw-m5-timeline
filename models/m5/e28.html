Design Legacy

Subtle exterior enhancements marked the E28 M5 as something special, while maintaining the sleeper aesthetic that would become an M5 trademark.

The First M5

Hand-built in Garching by BMW M GmbH, the E28 M5 was the fastest production sedan in the world when it launched in 1984. Each car was meticulously assembled by skilled technicians, taking two weeks to complete. With the M1's racing-derived engine, it created the blueprint for all future M5 generations. Only 2,241 units were ever produced, making it one of the rarest and most collectible M cars.

  • Hand-built at BMW M GmbH in Garching
  • World's fastest production sedan in 1984
  • Two-week build process per car
  • Extremely limited production numbers

M1 Supercar DNA

The E28 M5's heart was a modified version of the legendary BMW M1 supercar engine. This sophisticated 3.5-liter inline-six featured individual throttle bodies, four valves per cylinder, and Bosch Motronic fuel injection. The M88/3 engine was a masterpiece of engineering, delivering remarkable performance through its 6-speed manual transmission.

  • M1-derived M88/3 engine
  • Individual throttle bodies
  • Four valves per cylinder
  • Bosch Motronic injection

Stealth Performance

The E28 M5 established the blueprint for the modern super sedan with its understated appearance hiding extraordinary performance. Subtle exterior modifications included a front air dam, rear spoiler, and unique M-specific wheels. The chassis was enhanced with M-tuned suspension, larger brakes, and a limited-slip differential.

  • Subtle exterior enhancements
  • M-tuned suspension
  • Limited-slip differential
  • Enhanced braking system

Historical Significance

The E28 M5 remains one of the most significant cars in BMW's history, marking the beginning of the high-performance luxury sedan segment. Its combination of supercar performance, everyday usability, and understated design created a template that continues to influence performance cars today.
