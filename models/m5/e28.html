BMW E28 M5: The First M5 Ever - Complete History & Guide

The BMW E28 M5, produced from 1984 to 1988, stands as one of the most iconic and significant automobiles in BMW's history. As the first-ever M5, this legendary hand-built performance sedan launched what would become one of the most respected nameplates in the automotive world. With only 2,241 units produced, the E28 M5 represents automotive craftsmanship at its finest.

BMW E28 M5 Engine Specifications & Performance

The heart of the E28 M5 is the legendary 3.5-liter S38 inline-six engine, hand-built at BMW M GmbH in Garching, Germany. This naturally aspirated powerplant produces an impressive 286 horsepower and 251 lb-ft of torque, making it one of the most potent six-cylinder engines of its era. The S38 engine would later become famous for powering the BMW M1 supercar, lending the E28 M5 genuine motorsport DNA.

With a 0-60 mph acceleration time of just 6.5 seconds and a top speed of 153 mph (246 km/h), the E28 M5 was genuinely quick for 1984. These performance figures were extraordinary for a four-door sedan at the time, establishing the M5 as a true performance icon. The engine features Bosch Motronic fuel injection, ensuring reliability and consistency.

E28 M5 Design & Technical Innovation

Visually, the E28 M5 is characterized by its understated elegance. The design brief was to create a true sleeper - a car that wouldn't announce its performance capabilities immediately. The body sits on the iconic E28 3 Series platform but features reinforced chassis components, sport suspension tuning, and brake upgrades specifically for M5 duty.

Key technical features include a Getrag 260/6 five-speed manual transmission (the only gearbox option), independent double-wishbone front suspension with coil springs, and a solid rear axle with coil springs. The braking system features larger ventilated discs all around, a significant upgrade for the era. The E28 M5 also introduced the iconic M-Technic bodykit with subtle aero enhancements and distinctive 16-inch M wheels.

Production Numbers & Rarity

Between September 1984 and June 1988, BMW M GmbH hand-assembled exactly 2,241 E28 M5 units. Each car was built with meticulous attention to detail, with final assembly and testing done by hand. This limited production makes the E28 M5 one of the rarest M5 generations ever built. The numbers break down as follows: 1,400 left-hand drive models for continental Europe and other markets, and 841 right-hand drive models for UK, Ireland, and select other markets.

The extreme rarity of the E28 M5, combined with its historical significance as the first M5 ever produced, has made it one of the most sought-after classic BMWs among collectors worldwide. Every E28 M5 carries a build number and detailed service history documentation, adding to its collectible value.

BMW E28 M5 Market Value & Pricing

The BMW E28 M5 price depends heavily on condition, mileage, service history, and location. Current market values (as of 2025) range from $50,000 for project cars to $150,000 for concours-condition examples with full documentation. Well-preserved examples with complete service records typically command $85,000 to $120,000.

In Europe, E28 M5 prices are generally higher due to better availability. UK right-hand drive examples range from £35,000 to £85,000. In India and South Asia, E28 M5 availability is extremely limited, with import regulations making acquisition challenging. The appreciating market for classic BMWs suggests E28 M5 values will continue to rise as fewer original examples survive.

E28 M5 Maintenance Costs & Ownership

Owning an E28 M5 requires commitment to proper maintenance. The S38 engine, while reliable, demands high-quality synthetic oils and regular service intervals (every 5,000 miles is recommended). Annual maintenance costs typically range from $1,500 to $3,000 depending on the car's condition and whether major repairs are needed.

The Getrag transmission is known for durability but fluid changes should be performed every 30,000 miles. Suspension components, particularly the solid rear axle bushings, may require replacement depending on age and use. Finding original BMW M parts can be challenging, though many components are interchangeable with other E28 models. Specialist BMW mechanics familiar with classic M cars are essential for proper maintenance.

E28 M5 vs M535i: Key Differences

During its production run, BMW also offered the M535i, which used a 218-horsepower version of the 3.5-liter engine with different tuning. The E28 M5 features the higher-performance 286-horsepower variant with revised engine management, upgraded fuel injection, and enhanced internal components. Only the M5 version received the full M treatment with revised suspension, brakes, and exterior styling.

The M535i is often confused with the E28 M5 but commands significantly lower prices due to its lesser performance credentials and larger production numbers. True M5 enthusiasts and collectors prioritize authentic E28 M5 examples with complete original documentation.

Collecting the BMW E28 M5: Investment Potential

The E28 M5 represents one of BMW's most significant collector cars with strong appreciation potential. Several factors contribute to its investment appeal:

  • Historical Significance: First-ever M5, launching one of automotive's most revered lineages
  • Limited Production: Only 2,241 units worldwide, making it rarer than most exotic sports cars
  • Hand-Built Heritage: Each car meticulously assembled at BMW M GmbH
  • M1-Derived Engine: S38 engine shares DNA with BMW's legendary supercar
  • Growing Classic Market: Consistent appreciation in values as collectors recognize significance
  • Original Specifications: Opportunity to preserve or restore original examples

Market data suggests E28 M5 values have appreciated 8-12% annually over the past decade. Well-maintained examples with original specifications and complete service history are most sought-after by collectors.

BMW E28 M5 Left-Hand Drive vs Right-Hand Drive

Production split between left-hand drive (1,400 units) and right-hand drive (841 units) models affects pricing and availability. Right-hand drive examples, produced for UK and Irish markets, are particularly sought-after in those regions. Left-hand drive examples dominate European markets and America. Both configurations have equal historical value, though rarity in specific regions can influence local market pricing.

Why the BMW E28 M5 Matters in Automotive History

The E28 M5 changed the automotive industry's perception of what a sedan could be. Before the M5, performance cars meant two-seater sports cars or muscle cars. BMW proved that a practical, four-door family sedan could deliver supercar-beating performance while maintaining comfort and daily usability.

This philosophy established the template for the modern high-performance sedan category that continues today. Every high-performance sedan on the road today - from the Audi RS6 to the Mercedes-AMG E63 - owes a debt to the E28 M5's pioneering concept. The car's success spawned six subsequent generations, each pushing performance boundaries further while maintaining the M5's core identity as the ultimate performance sedan.

Buying Guide: What to Look For in an E28 M5

Service History: Complete documented service history is essential. Original BMW service records add significant value. Verify that major maintenance has been performed according to schedule.

Rust Assessment: Check undercarriage, rear panels, and floor pans carefully. Rust is the enemy of older BMWs. Look for evidence of rust repair or welding.

Engine Condition: The S38 engine should start easily and run smoothly. Listen for unusual noises. Request a pre-purchase inspection by a BMW M specialist.

Original Parts: Verify originality of major components. Original interior trim, gauges, and badging are more valuable than replacements.

Mileage: Lower mileage examples command premium prices. However, cars with regular use and proper maintenance may be more reliable than low-mileage garage queens.

Color & Interior: Original color combinations are more desirable. Common colors like black and silver are typical. Rare colors can add value.

The Legacy of the BMW E28 M5

Four decades after its introduction, the BMW E28 M5 remains a benchmark for what a high-performance sedan should be. Its combination of hand-built quality, motorsport heritage, limited production, and genuine performance continues to captivate enthusiasts and collectors worldwide.

As the original and rarest M5 ever produced, the E28 has earned its place in automotive history. Whether as an investment, a collector's piece, or simply a driver's car, the E28 M5 represents peak BMW performance engineering from the 1980s - proof that the marque's commitment to performance predates the modern hypercar era by decades.

For collectors seeking an E28 M5, research thoroughly, inspect carefully, and work with BMW specialists. An original, well-maintained example represents one of the finest investments in classic automotive excellence.
