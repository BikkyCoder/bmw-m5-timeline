Two Hearts, One Soul

The E34 M5 was offered with two different S38 engines: the original 3.6L with 311 hp, and from 1991, an enlarged 3.8L version producing 340 hp. Both maintained the M5's position as one of the world's fastest sedans. With 12,254 units produced (including both sedan and touring variants), it marked a significant expansion in M5 production.

Hand-Built Legacy

As the last hand-built M5, each E34 M5 was assembled at BMW M's Garching facility following the "one man, one engine" philosophy. It introduced several innovations including Electronic Damper Control (EDC), improved aerodynamics, and was the first M5 available as a touring wagon, expanding the model's versatility while maintaining its performance heritage.

Innovations

The E34 M5 introduced several technological advances including Electronic Damper Control (EDC), improved aerodynamics, and a more sophisticated engine management system. It was also the last hand-built M5, with each car being assembled at BMW M's Garching facility.

Legacy

As the last hand-built M5 and the first to be offered as a wagon, the E34 M5 holds a special place in BMW M history. Its combination of refinement, performance, and exclusivity set new standards for luxury performance cars.

The E34 M5 marked the end of an era as the last hand-assembled M5, with each engine being built by a single master technician following BMW M's "one man, one engine" philosophy.
