BMW F90 M5 (2017-2023): The First AWD M5 - Complete Guide to Specs, Pricing & Performance

The BMW F90 M5, produced from 2017 to 2023, represents a revolutionary transformation in BMW M5 history as the first generation to feature AWD (all-wheel drive) technology through the sophisticated M xDrive system. This seven-year production run introduced the powerful S63TU4 twin-turbo V8 engine in multiple power outputs: 600 hp standard, 625 hp Competition variant, and 635 hp flagship CS model. The F90 M5 marked a new era combining modern technology, luxury appointments, and genuine performance, making it highly accessible and desirable. Approximately 80,000+ units were produced worldwide during this generation, making the F90 M5 significantly more available than earlier generations while maintaining strong collector and performance potential, particularly in the Indian market.

BMW F90 M5 S63TU4 Twin-Turbo V8 Engine Specifications

The BMW F90 M5 features the sophisticated S63TU4 twin-turbo V8 engine, a 4.4-liter (4,395cc) displacement engine with advanced twin-turbocharged technology, producing 625 horsepower and 750 Nm (553 lb-ft) of torque in Competition form. The flagship M5 CS variant increases output to 635 horsepower with identical torque specifications. The S63TU4 features advanced engine management, direct fuel injection, variable VANOS timing on both intake and exhaust camshafts, and sophisticated electronic turbo management for minimal lag and maximum responsiveness.

The engine architecture utilizes a lightweight aluminum block with cross-bolted main bearing caps for rigidity and reliability. Forged steel pistons and high-strength connecting rods handle turbocharger boost pressures reliably. The S63TU4 produces 100% of peak torque from just 1,150 RPM, delivering devastating low-end power that enables the F90 M5 to accelerate from standstill with exceptional authority. The twin-IHI turbochargers with variable turbine geometry (VTG) ensure minimal turbo lag while optimizing efficiency across the entire RPM range. This combination of advanced turbocharger technology and refined engine management makes the S63TU4 one of the most capable engines in BMW M's history.

BMW F90 M5 Performance & Acceleration Specifications

The BMW F90 M5 Competition achieves 0-100 km/h acceleration in just 3.3 seconds, making it one of the fastest production sedans ever created. The flagship M5 CS achieves an extraordinary 0-100 km/h in 3.0 seconds - faster than most supercars of its era. These exceptional performance times stem from the S63TU4's 625-635 hp combined with the sophisticated M xDrive AWD system's launch control optimization. The 0-60 mph times (3.2 seconds Competition, 2.8 seconds CS) establish the F90 M5 as a genuine supercar rival, competitive with Lamborghini and Ferrari performance.

Quarter-mile performance for the M5 Competition stands at approximately 11.9 seconds with terminal velocity exceeding 190 km/h, demonstrating exceptional sustained acceleration beyond just launch performance. The top speed is electronically limited to 250 km/h for safety reasons. However, when delimited through professional ECU tuning, the F90 M5 Competition achieves approximately 307+ km/h (190+ mph), while the M5 CS reaches 320+ km/h (198+ mph) on appropriate closed circuits. With M Driver's Package and performance settings optimization, acceleration and top speed capabilities can be further enhanced.

BMW F90 M5 AWD & M xDrive System Technology

The F90 M5 pioneered the first all-wheel-drive M5 generation through the innovative M xDrive system, a sophisticated electronically-controlled AWD platform providing multiple driving modes. The system allows drivers to select between 4WD (full-time AWD for safety and traction), 4WD Sport (optimized for performance), and exclusive 2WD (rear-wheel drive only for maximum engagement and drifting). This switchable architecture maintains the M5's driver-focused character while providing modern all-weather capability.

The M xDrive system uses an electronically-controlled differential managing power distribution between front and rear axles in real-time, responding to driving conditions, throttle input, and steering angle. Launch control integration ensures optimal traction from standstill, while dynamic stability management operates seamlessly across all driving modes. The 8-speed M DCT (dual-clutch transmission) delivers torque with exceptional efficiency, enabling the F90 M5 to achieve class-leading acceleration times while maintaining reliability even under extreme driving conditions.

BMW F90 M5 Engine Oil & Maintenance Requirements

The F90 M5 requires exclusive use of BMW LL-04 5W-30 synthetic oil, representing a significant departure from earlier 10W-60 requirements. Total oil capacity is 8.5 liters. The oil change interval is every 10,000 km or annually, whichever comes first. Using BMW LL-04 specification is mandatory - alternative oils may damage the advanced turbocharger system and void warranty. In India, BMW LL-04 5W-30 costs approximately ₹2,800 per liter, making each oil change service approximately ₹20,000-25,000 including the oil filter (₹1,200).

Annual maintenance costs in India typically range from ₹1.2-1.5 lakhs for regular service including oil changes and inspections. Major services every three years cost ₹2-2.5 lakhs including transmission fluid changes and brake fluid replacement. Spark plug replacement costs ₹40,000-60,000. M Driver's Package brake fluid replacement: ₹30,000-50,000. Transmission service: ₹50,000-1 lakh every 60,000 km. For cars in India, authorized BMW service centers provide comprehensive warranty packages covering preventive maintenance.

BMW F90 M5 CS vs Competition vs Standard Variants

BMW F90 M5 Fuel Efficiency & Real-World Mileage

BMW F90 M5 Buying Guide: Essential Pre-Purchase Considerations

Service History: Complete authorized BMW service records are absolutely essential. Verify oil changes every 10,000 km with BMW LL-04 5W-30 oil specifically. Check major service documentation including transmission fluid changes and brake fluid replacement. Request the vehicle's complete service booklet.

Engine & Turbo Health: Request compression testing and boost pressure verification from authorized BMW specialists. Verify smooth turbo response without hesitation or noise. Have diagnostic scan performed checking turbo actuator status and engine management systems.

M xDrive System: Verify all drive modes (4WD, 4WD Sport, 2WD) function smoothly. Test launch control and traction management in multiple modes. Check for any warning messages related to drivetrain systems.

Transmission & Chassis: ZF 8-speed DCT should shift smoothly and responsively. Test paddle shifters and manual mode functionality. Verify M Dynamic Drive suspension operates properly across all modes. Suspension geometry inspection recommended.

BMW F90 M5 Historical Significance & Legacy

The F90 M5 marks a revolutionary turning point in BMW M5 history as the first generation to feature all-wheel drive through the M xDrive system. This represented BMW's adaptation to modern performance demands while maintaining the M5's driver-focused character through switchable 2WD capability. The F90 M5 established templates for subsequent hybrid and electric M5 generations, making it a bridge between pure performance tradition and the modern electrified era.

The F90 M5 represents the optimal modern performance sedan, offering the technology, luxury, and connectivity demanded by contemporary buyers while maintaining genuine supercar performance. Today, as earlier naturally-aspirated and turbocharged M5 generations become increasingly collectible, the F90 M5 represents exceptional value and practicality - offering modern technology, warranty potential, and reliability at a fraction of earlier generation prices.
